# Audit checklist for existing case studies

Use when reworking existing portfolio pieces. Score each criterion: OK / Weak / Missing. Present findings in priority order (Tier 1 first), with a concrete fix for each, then interview to fill gaps and rewrite.

## Tier 1: Deal-breakers (fix these first)

1. **Result visible in the first screen?** The headline outcome must appear in the hero/summary, not at the bottom. Missing results is the single most common reason a case study fails.
2. **Role unambiguous?** Can a reviewer state in one sentence what Herman personally did? "We"-language without an "I" is a red flag for reviewers.
3. **Problem framed with stakes?** Is it clear why the problem mattered to the client/users, or does it just say "they wanted a new website"?
4. **Any "why" at all?** Are decisions explained, or is it a list of deliverables and screens? Screens without reasoning is the #1 reviewer complaint.
5. **Scannable in 2 minutes?** Clear headings, short paragraphs, visual hierarchy. Walls of text kill case studies.

## Tier 2: What separates good from great

6. **Friction shown?** At least one problem, trade-off, or course-correction with how it was handled. No friction reads as fabricated or shallow.
7. **Process evidence?** Wireframes, sketches, iterations, research notes. Proof of work, not just final polish.
8. **Constraints stated?** Budget, timeline, tech, client factors. Decisions only demonstrate judgment when the constraints are visible.
9. **Curated visuals?** Only screens that carry the story, each with an explanatory caption. No screen-dumping.
10. **Reflection present?** Brief, honest, specific lessons. Signals maturity.

## Tier 3: Polish

11. **Title jargon-free?** Understandable to a non-technical recruiter in one read.
12. **Consistent with other case studies?** Same structure, image treatment, tone across the portfolio.
13. **Image quality?** Sharp, consistent framing, optimized formats, proper alt text.
14. **Length?** Readable in ~5 minutes. If longer, one thread is usually trying to do too much; cut secondary threads to a sentence each.
15. **Metrics defensible?** Every number can be explained in an interview. Remove anything inflated or unverifiable.
16. **Live link works?** If the project is live, it's linked and the link is current.
17. **Both languages?** NO and EN versions exist and are equivalent in quality (not one polished, one machine-translated).

## Presenting the audit

Format findings as a short prioritized list, not a table dump. For each issue: what's wrong, why it costs (tie to reviewer behavior), and the concrete fix. Then ask which gaps the user can fill with more information, and run the relevant interview questions from `interview-questions.md` before rewriting.

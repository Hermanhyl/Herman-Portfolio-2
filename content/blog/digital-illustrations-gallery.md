While my professional focus is frontend development and UI/UX design, digital illustration has been a lifelong passion that continues to influence my visual design work. My portfolio includes over 80 illustrations spanning various styles and subjects.

## The Categories

### Portraits
Portrait work has always been central to my illustration practice. From celebrity studies like David Bowie and Daniel Radcliffe to original character portraits, capturing likeness and personality in digital form is endlessly challenging and rewarding. Self-portraits serve as both practice and artistic expression.

### Characters
Character design lets imagination run free. My character work ranges from superheroes like Batman and Black Panther to original concepts—cowboys, sailors, angels, and everything in between. Each character presents unique challenges in pose, expression, and storytelling.

### Dragons & Creatures
Fantasy creatures, especially dragons, are among my favorite subjects. From the majestic Elder Dragon to playful young dragons breathing their first fire, these pieces combine anatomy studies with pure imagination. The Phoenix and other mythical creatures also make appearances.

### Knights & Warriors
Medieval and fantasy warriors offer rich opportunities for armor design, dramatic poses, and storytelling through visual detail. Whether it's a knight in a red cape, a female warrior in spartan-inspired armor, or quick character sketches, these pieces explore themes of strength and heroism.

### Animals & Birds
Nature provides endless inspiration. Eagles, ravens, owls, and other birds appear frequently, along with mammals like dogs and snakes. These studies help maintain fundamental drawing skills while celebrating the natural world.

### Landscapes & Scenes
From space stations to cherry blossom scenes, environmental work challenges me to think about composition, perspective, and atmosphere. These pieces often serve as backgrounds for character work or standalone explorations of mood and setting.

### Sketches & Studies
Not every piece is finished. My sketch and study work shows the process behind polished illustrations—perspective training, pose studies, gradient experiments, and concept explorations. These "behind the scenes" pieces are essential practice.

## Tools & Process

All my digital illustration work is created using:

- **Procreate** on iPad for the majority of pieces
- **Adobe Photoshop** for complex compositions
- Various brushes mimicking traditional media

The digital medium allows for experimentation without material costs, making it ideal for trying new techniques and styles.

## How Illustration Informs My Development Work

My illustration background directly influences my frontend work:

### Color Sense
Years of color mixing and palette selection transfer directly to UI design. Understanding how colors interact helps create harmonious, accessible interfaces.

### Composition
Illustration composition principles—balance, focal points, visual flow—apply equally to web layouts and component design.

### Visual Problem-Solving
Both illustration and coding require breaking complex problems into manageable pieces. The creative thinking developed through art applies to technical challenges.

### Attention to Detail
Illustration trains you to notice subtle details—lighting, proportion, consistency. This attention carries over to pixel-perfect UI implementation.

## Continuing the Journey

Illustration remains an active part of my creative life. Each piece teaches something new, and the skills developed through art continue to enhance my work as a developer and designer.
